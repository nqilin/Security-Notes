# What is Cybersecurity
## Core Concept
Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computer systems, networks, and data from digital attacks, theft, or damage. It focuses on keeping information confidential, available, and intact.

## Why It Matters
Every individual and organization uses digital systems—cybersecurity prevents data breaches, financial loss, and reputational damage. Without it, personal info (like passwords, bank details) and business data are at risk.

## How It Works (Theory)
1. Identify assets (data, systems, networks) that need protection.
2. Assess potential threats (hackers, malware, human error) and vulnerabilities.
3. Implement security measures to defend assets.
4. Monitor and update defenses to adapt to new threats.

## Practical Example
A small business uses a password manager for employee accounts, enables two-factor authentication (2FA), and updates its software regularly—these are basic cybersecurity practices to prevent unauthorized access.

## How to Defend / Secure
1. Keep software (OS, apps) up to date (patches fix vulnerabilities).
2. Use strong, unique passwords and 2FA.
3. Be cautious of phishing emails (avoid clicking unknown links/attachments).
4. Back up important data regularly.

## Key Takeaways
1. Cybersecurity protects digital assets from various threats.
2. It’s a continuous process, not a one-time setup.
3. Basic practices (updates, strong passwords) go a long way for beginners.
