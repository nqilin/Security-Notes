# Attack Types Overview
## Core Concept
Cybersecurity attacks are methods used by threat actors to compromise systems or steal data. This note covers the most common attack types for beginners.

## Why It Matters
Recognizing common attack types helps you spot warning signs and take preventive measures. You don’t need to be an expert—just know what to look for.

## How It Works (Theory)
Common attack types and their logic:
1. Phishing: Tricking users into sharing sensitive info (e.g., fake email from a bank).
2. Malware: Malicious software (viruses, ransomware) that infects systems.
3. Man-in-the-Middle (MitM): Intercepting communication between two parties (e.g., spy on public Wi-Fi).
4. Brute Force: Guessing passwords or encryption keys by trying all possible combinations.

## Practical Example
- Phishing: You receive an email saying “Your bank account is locked—click here to reset your password” (link leads to a fake bank website).
- Ransomware (a type of malware): A virus encrypts your files and demands money to unlock them.

## How to Defend / Secure
1. Phishing: Verify senders before clicking links/attaching files; use email filters.
2. Malware: Install antivirus software; avoid downloading files from untrusted sources.
3. MitM: Use HTTPS/VPN on public Wi-Fi; avoid sharing sensitive info on public networks.
4. Brute Force: Use strong passwords; enable account lockout after failed attempts.

## Key Takeaways
1. Most attacks target human error (phishing) or unpatched systems (malware).
2. Simple precautions (verify links, use antivirus) can block 80% of common attacks.
3. If something seems too good to be true (e.g., free gift), it’s likely a phishing attempt.
